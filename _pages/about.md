I am Tien Cuong Phi, a researcher at Kyoto University, Japan, working at the intersection of probability, statistics, and computational neuroscience. My work develops mathematical and computational tools to model, simulate, and interpret the activity of large-scale neuronal systems.

My research spans stochastic modelling of point processes and the simulation of potentially infinite neuronal networks, the reconstruction of functional connectivity from neural data, and, more recently, the study of perception and consciousness through integrated information theory and Bayesian modelling of brain activity. I am particularly interested in bridging rigorous probabilistic theory with data-driven methods to uncover the mechanisms underlying neural computation.

I hold a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Université Côte d'Azur, France, where I was advised by Patricia Reynaud-Bouret and Eva Löcherbach, following earlier studies in Nice and at Hanoi National University of Education, Vietnam. A complete overview of my background is available on my [CV]({{ base_path }}/cv/).
